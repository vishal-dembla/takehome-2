Take-Home Salary in India: CTC vs In-Hand Salary (₹5 Lakh to ₹20 Lakh)

One of the most common questions professionals ask when receiving a job offer is simple: “If my CTC is ₹X lakh, how much will I actually take home?”

Unfortunately, understanding your in-hand salary in India isn’t always straightforward. Between taxes, PF contributions, deductions, and the choice between Old and New Tax Regimes, take-home pay can vary even with the same gross CTC.

This guide gives you a clear, realistic comparison of CTC vs. take-home salary for annual packages ranging from ₹5 lakh to ₹20 lakh, based on typical salary structures in India.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

1. Why is my take-home less than my CTC?
Because your CTC includes non-cash benefits and deductions like employer's PF contribution, gratuity, insurance, and taxes.

2. Does Old vs. New Tax Regime impact my take-home?
Yes. From ₹8L upwards, the New Regime often offers better take-home unless you have significant deductions like a housing loan or maxed-out 80C investments.

3. Are bonuses included in this table?
No. This only shows fixed monthly salaries, excluding performance bonuses or incentives.

4. How can I get a good estimate?
Use a detailed take-home salary calculator that factors in the tax regime, PF choices, and your location to provide a realistic breakdown.
